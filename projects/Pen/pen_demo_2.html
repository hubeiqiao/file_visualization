Summary

Dynamicland is a nonprofit lab that creates a communal computing environment for people to explore ideas together in the real world. Instead of using personal computers, they encourage collaboration through physical materials and hands-on activities. Their goal is to foster a new form of literacy that helps communities understand and address complex issues together.

What is Dynamicland?

Nonprofit Research Lab

Creating a humane dynamic medium where computation extends beyond screens into real-world interaction, focusing on tangible, communal computing experiences.

Community Focused

A public space for hands-on learning that brings people together for collaboration in the same physical space, enabling spontaneous interactions and knowledge sharing.

Key Concepts

Real-World Computation

  • Uses physical materials, not just screens
  • Programs exist on objects—tables, walls, etc.
  • Enabled by projectors, cameras, and sensors
  • Computation like electric lighting—wherever needed

Communal Computing

  • People together, hands-on collaboration
  • Like a woodshop or communal kitchen
  • Work is visible—everyone learns from each other
  • Spontaneous collaborations emerge naturally

Authoring & Programs

  • Not just for developers—anyone can create
  • Programs are physical things, visual and editable
  • Learning by doing—experimentation over coding
  • Programs are simple, leveraging real-world physics

Realtalk (OS of Dynamicland)

  • No codebase → programs exist as physical boards
  • Self-hosted, no laptops required
  • Complete computing system built in itself
  • Open, visible, and editable by anyone

Agency & Literacy

  • Computation as literacy, not a product!
  • Communities build & control their own computation
  • 21st-century literacy needed for real-world problems
  • Computation should be taught & learned, not downloaded

Community

  • Public space for hands-on learning
  • Covers broad topics, now focused on biosciences
  • Diverse participants: artists, scientists, educators
  • Creating communal science environments
