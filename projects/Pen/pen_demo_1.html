What is Dynamicland?

A nonprofit research lab creating a humane dynamic medium for real-world computation.

Physical Computing

Working out in the real world with physical materials, putting computational programs onto ordinary objects.

Communal Experience

People coming together in the same space, getting their hands on the same thing, collaborating spontaneously.

Editable Programs

Programs are visible things on the table or wall that anyone can change at any time and see the changes immediately.

How It Works: Realtalk

Realtalk is not a codebase, but a physical system - a poster gallery or bulletin board that everyone can see and change at any time.

  • Built entirely in Realtalk itself, out in the open with real materials
  • No laptops needed - a complete self-hosted computing system
  • Programs can be in language, arrangements of components, or hand-drawn diagrams
  • Simplicity is key - the real world simulates itself, so programs can be small and simple

Current Focus: Biosciences

After COVID, Dynamicland moved into integrating communal computing into an existing community of bioscientists, building a vision of communal science where:

  • People from different fields work together in the same space
  • Building models together and learning each other's expertise
  • Computation integrated with physical experiments

The Bigger Picture

"Not a product, but a practice."

"Democracy requires universal literacy. And democracy in the 21st century is going to require a new kind of literacy, for understanding and discussing systems that can't be captured in words and pictures."

Community-Built Computing

Dynamicland is nonprofit, and Realtalk is not a product. The goal is to invent a form of computation which local communities of non-specialists can make for themselves.

Built from the ground up, for their own needs, which they fully understand and control.

21st-Century Literacy

We need new tools for understanding and discussing complex systems like the environment, equity, health, and global economies.

These problems will be solved by real people gathering together, using computation to explore ideas in the physical world.
