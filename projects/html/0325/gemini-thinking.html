Part 1: Foundation of Scoping Reviews

Glossary

Term Definition
Boolean operators Logical connectors (AND, OR, NOT) used in database searches to refine search results.
Grey literature Information produced outside traditional publishing channels, like reports and proceedings.
Human oversight Human judgment in automated systems to ensure ethical and responsible outcomes.
PRISMA-ScR Reporting guidelines for scoping reviews.
Scoping review Literature review mapping concepts and research gaps, without quality assessment.
Systematic review Structured review of research on a specific question, with rigorous methodology.

Introduction

Scoping reviews gather and examine a wide range of sources to show what is known and unknown, especially valuable in Technology Innovation Management (TIM) for identifying opportunities in areas like blockchain, open innovation, and AI startups. They cast a wider net than systematic reviews, suitable for rapidly evolving technology landscapes. If your review is narrowly defined and quality-focused, a systematic review is more appropriate.

1.1 When to Use a Scoping Review Instead of a Systematic Review

In TIM, scoping reviews are particularly useful when:

  • Broad or Exploratory Questions: Explore a wide variety of studies on a topic. Example: AI platforms in technology commercialization.
  • Emerging Topics: When the field is new and concepts are unclear. Example: Quantum computing applications in startups.
  • Heterogeneous Literature: Diverse methodologies and disciplines. Example: AI in healthcare, finance, manufacturing.
  • Minimal Critical Appraisal: Cataloging strategies rather than evaluating effectiveness. Example: Open innovation strategies.
  • Identifying Gaps and Trends: Summarize research and inform future directions. Example: Blockchain in supply chain management.

1.2 Key Frameworks

Scoping reviews in TIM often follow these frameworks:

  • Arksey and O’Malley (2005): Flexible, good for fast-changing fields. Example: Wearable technology for patient monitoring.
  • Levac et al. (2010): Emphasizes stakeholder engagement, vital for TIM. Example: IoT in smart manufacturing with startup accelerators.
  • Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI): Rigorous methodology, good for diverse sources. Example: 3D-printing technologies for rapid prototyping.
  • PRISMA-ScR: Standardized reporting, enhances credibility. Example: Blockchain in supply chain management reporting.
  • Recent Updates (2020–Present): Incorporate AI tools for speed and data analysis. Example: AI in digital entrepreneurship post-COVID-19.

1.3 Benefits of Using AI for Scoping Reviews in TIM Context

AI manages large data volumes quickly and consistently, speeding up research and delivering actionable insights in TIM.

  • Speed and Efficiency: Rapid literature screening, automated deduplication.
  • Enhanced Discovery: Diverse data sources, intelligent recommendations.
  • Improved Organization: Topic clustering, automatic tagging & categorization.
  • Scalable Analysis: Big data handling, dynamic updating.
  • Reduced Human Error: Consistent screening criteria, continuous refinement.
  • Insight Generation: Text summarization, trend detection.
  • Cost Savings: Streamlined workflows, resource allocation.

1.4 Limitations of Scoping Reviews

While scoping reviews offer broad overviews, they have limitations:

  • Lack of Critical Appraisal: May include low-quality studies.
  • Broad and Less Focused Scope: Insufficient depth for specific recommendations.
  • No Formal Strength-of-Evidence Assessment: Difficult to uniformly assess quality.
  • Selection Bias: Risk of overemphasizing highly cited clusters.
  • Heterogeneity of Studies: Combining diverse data complicates analysis.
  • Lack of Meta-Analysis: No effect size calculations.
  • Time and Resource-Intensive: Mining complex domains requires extensive searching.
  • Difficulty in Defining Scope: Balancing coverage with feasibility is tricky.
  • Risk of Overlooking Grey Literature: Missing real-world insights.
  • Challenges in Updating: Rapid technology shifts require frequent re-scoping.

1.5 Limitations of Using AI Tools to Produce Scoping Reviews

AI tools improve productivity but require caution:

  • 1.5.1 AI-created mistakes in citations – ‘hallucinations’: AI might invent references. Mitigation: Double-check citations.
  • 1.5.2 Lack of Contextual Understanding: AI may oversimplify nuances. Mitigation: Human expertise, validate with experts.
  • 1.5.3 Bias in AI-Generated Content: AI may favor Anglophone research. Mitigation: Diverse database searches, triangulate sources.
  • 1.5.4 Overreliance on Recent Literature: AI might miss foundational works. Mitigation: Manual backward citation, scan foundational papers.
  • 1.5.5 Difficulty Managing Grey Literature: AI overlooks industry reports. Mitigation: Targeted grey literature searches, manual assessment.
  • 1.5.6 Inconsistent Handling of Research Methodologies: AI may misclassify study types. Mitigation: Manual confirmation, expert appraisal.
  • 1.5.7 Ethical Concerns and Plagiarism Risks: AI text might resemble existing literature. Mitigation: Cite sources, plagiarism tools, ethics guidelines.

1.6 AI Biases

AI biases can shape understanding of technology trends:

  • 1.6.1 Selection bias: Overrepresentation of visible sources. Mitigation: Extend search to regional databases.
  • 1.6.2 Algorithmic bias: AI may amplify established perspectives. Mitigation: Use multiple AI tools, cross-verify.
  • 1.6.3 Confirmation bias: AI may align with initial search terms. Mitigation: Include counter-narratives, critical examination.
  • 1.6.4 Citation bias: AI prioritizes widely cited older work. Mitigation: Update search parameters, include novel studies.
  • 1.6.5 Language and Accessibility Bias: AI focuses on English open-access. Mitigation: Multilingual databases, translations.
  • 1.6.6 Data Hallucination: AI might merge concepts fictitiously. Mitigation: Verify references, manual investigation.

1.7 Human Oversight

Human oversight in TIM scoping reviews is crucial for:

  • Monitoring AI outputs for domain accuracy.
  • Evaluating study suitability and credibility.
  • Intervening when AI misinterprets concepts.

Blend AI efficiency with contextual knowledge of technology markets and regulations.

1.8 Step-by-step Approach to Grey Literature Searches

Grey literature reveals real-world insights missed by journals. Steps include:

  1. Define Scope: Identify relevant technology reports, policy documents, whitepapers.
  2. Pick Keywords: Outline core TIM concepts and synonyms with Boolean operators.
  3. Select Sources: Government databases, think tanks, industry reports (Gartner, Forrester, CB Insights), patent databases, conference proceedings, institutional repositories.
  4. Search Specialized Databases: OpenGrey, NTIS, ProQuest Dissertations, Google Scholar (filters).
  5. Search Competitor Info: Company websites, press releases, SEC filings, specialized databases (Crunchbase, PitchBook).
  6. Contact Experts: Government agencies, incubators, associations, LinkedIn, TIM conferences.
  7. Filter and Evaluate: Authority, credibility, relevance, timeliness.

1.9 Reference Management

Organize sources effectively for scoping reviews. Reference managers help with:

  • Efficient organization
  • Automated citation formatting
  • Collaboration and sharing
  • Duplicate detection
  • Integration with writing software

Recommended managers: Zotero (webpage capture, Google Docs integration, AI plugins), Mendeley (AI suggestions, PDF annotation), EndNote (large-scale data, ResearchRabbit integration).

2.1 Step 1: Formulate the Review Question and Scope

Requires a broad and exploratory review question. Use AI (ChatGPT) to generate variations and refine clarity. Start broad, refine iteratively. AI identifies gaps and key authors. Human oversight ensures clarity, feasibility, and theoretical frameworks. Guidance: compare AI suggestions with published reviews, align with frameworks like PCC (Population, Concept, Context).

Population, Concept, Context (PCC) Framework

Ensures clarity in scoping review formulation.

  • Example 1: Product development: Startups developing AI products; Challenges in scaling; Venture acceleration programs.
  • Example 2: Digital transformation in manufacturing: Mid-sized firms; Industry 4.0 adoption; North American landscape.
  • Example 3: Commercialization of quantum security: Cybersecurity firms, agencies; Strategies for quantum-resistant tech; National security context.
  • Example 4: Open Innovation in Corporate R&D: Large tech firms; Open innovation models; Global tech sector.
  • Example 5: Sustainability in supply chains: Tech hardware manufacturers; Sustainability practices; Global electronics industry.

Alternative Frameworks

  • CIMO (Context, Intervention, Mechanism, Outcome): For complex interventions. Example: AI project management software in remote startups.
  • ECLIPSE (Expectation, Client, Location, Impact, Professionals, Service): For technology-related services. Example: Cybersecurity risk frameworks for regulated tech firms.
  • PEO (Population, Exposure, Outcome): For qualitative research. Example: Remote work policies on software engineers.
  • SPIDER (Sample, Phenomenon, Design, Evaluation, Research type): For qualitative and mixed methods. Example: IT project managers experiencing agile methodology challenges.

Formulate Scope

Scope should be broad for key insights, specific for conclusions. Consider breadth vs. depth, time, literature type (academic vs. industry), geographical scope, and stakeholder perspective.

Outputs of Step 1

  • Clearly defined research question using PCC or other frameworks.
  • Review objectives outlining knowledge gaps.
  • Preliminary keywords and search terms.
  • Selection of search strategies and AI tools.
  • Defined scope with inclusion/exclusion criteria, source types, and relevant fields.

2.2 Step 2: Search for Articles

Thorough search is essential. AI helps generate keywords, Boolean strings, and identify databases. Human oversight refines queries, selects databases, and adjusts Boolean operators. Guidance: assess AI queries in multiple databases, validate keywords with prior reviews, include grey literature.

Develop Search Strategy

  • Define key terms and Boolean operators.
  • Adapt strategies for different databases.
  • Define inclusion/exclusion criteria.

Recommended AI Tools

  • ChatGPT: Generate search terms, Boolean strings, summarize themes and findings.
  • Consensus: Assess result relevance, rank papers, determine agreement.
  • Perplexity: Retrieve from academic/non-academic sources, generate answers from sources, latest research.
  • Elicit: Extract insights, prioritize studies, compare studies side-by-side.
  • OpenRead: Retrieve based on meaning, extract relevant parts, summarize papers.

Sources of Evidence & Databases

Combine peer-reviewed literature with industry insights. Use academic databases (Scopus, Web of Science, IEEE Xplore), industry reports (Gartner, McKinsey), preprints (ArXiv, SSRN), patent databases (Google Patents). Select databases aligned with research domain, considering indexing, quality, precision, and coverage. Databases include: Business & Tech (IEEE Xplore, ABI/INFORM), Multidisciplinary (Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar), Social Sciences (PsycINFO), Grey Literature (OpenGrey, OAIster), Health Sciences (PubMed).

  • Green IT and Sustainable Data Centers: Boolean Query and Filters example provided.

Manage Search Results & Document Process

  • Use reference management software, remove duplicates, track strategies, refine iteratively, troubleshoot if results too broad.
  • Document using PRISMA-ScR flow diagram, report dates/limits, justify selection criteria.

Outputs of Step 2

  • Documented search strategy.
  • Collection of relevant articles.
  • PRISMA-ScR flow diagram.
  • Refined search query.
  • List of excluded articles with reasons.

2.3 Step 3: Select Articles

Structured selection process is essential. Two-step screening: title/abstract and full-text. Use PRISMA-ScR flow diagram. AI (Rayyan) classifies, ranks, summarizes abstracts, detects duplicates. Human oversight reviews criteria, assesses relevance, identifies biases. Guidance: spot-checks, ensure key studies aren't omitted, dual human screening.

Screen Articles Strategy

  • Two Phases: Title/Abstract Screening (remove irrelevant studies using Rayyan AI), Full-Text Screening (assess alignment with criteria).
  • Title/Abstract Screening: Initial screening, apply criteria, use Rayyan for filtering, document changes, AI assists but doesn't replace manual.
  • Full-text screening: Retrieve full texts, assess criteria alignment, use Rayyan for tagging, justify exclusions.

Select Studies Using Rayyan AI

  • Upload & Organize References: Import from databases (RIS, BibTeX), set criteria, collaborative screening.
  • Screening: Prioritization, tagging, blind review mode.
  • Dual Screening: Two independent reviewers, minimizes bias, ensures reliability, resolution process for disagreements.
  • Final Selection: Export selected studies, organize in reference manager.

Track & Document

  • Maintain PRISMA-ScR flow diagram, record excluded articles with reasons, update screening process based on criteria evolution.

Outputs of Step 3

  • Refined set of eligible articles.
  • PRISMA-ScR flow diagram.
  • List of excluded articles with reasons.
  • Updated inclusion/exclusion criteria.
  • Title/Abstract and Full-Text screening results.

2.4 Step 4: Extract Data

Structured data extraction ensures systematic information capture. AI summarizes and organizes, manual oversight needed for accuracy. AI extracts themes, concepts, study characteristics, summarizes findings, groups studies. Human oversight verifies info, checks context, ensures methodological consistency. Guidance: manual sample extraction for AI accuracy comparison, use structured form (Excel, Covidence), align AI categories with review question.

Extract Key Information

  • Recommended AI Tools: ChatGPT 4.0, Elicit, SciSpace, Research Rabbit, Litmaps, Vos Viewer.
  • Key Data Elements: Citation details, study type, methodology, population, concepts, findings, gaps.
  • Adjust charting categories iteratively.

Extract Data & Organize into Tables

Recommended tools: Elicit or SciSpace for data table extraction. Alternatives: Retica, Scite Assistant, Diffbot, OutWit Hub. Extract study characteristics, key findings, research gaps. AI populates tables. Example table structures provided.

Visualize Insights

Reporting impact enhanced by clear visuals. Example: "AI in venture financing" insights. Recommended visualization tools: Concept Maps (CmapTools, MindMeister, Obsidian), AI-generated summaries (ChatGPT, Claude, Scite), Citation Networks (Research Rabbit, Litmaps, VOSviewer).

Ensure Data Quality & Consistency

  • Manually verify AI extractions, review charts for consistency, ethical review (AI summaries reflect original intent, JBI guidelines).

Document Extraction Process

  • Maintain data extraction template, track adjustments, ensure unbiased data handling.

Outputs of Step 4

  • Completed data extraction tables.
  • Structured dataset.
  • AI-generated and manually refined summaries.
  • Final data extraction template.
  • Records of methodological adjustments.

2.5 Step 5: Analyze and Synthesize Data

Goal is to organize, summarize, and present findings meaningfully. Prioritize mapping and thematic grouping over meta-analysis. AI summarizes themes, categorizes studies, generates thematic maps, identifies patterns. Human oversight verifies themes, ensures methodological consistency, assesses depth, refines categories, validates gaps. Guidance: read original studies to confirm AI accuracy, manually organize themes, refine AI categories, align with theoretical models, discuss with experts.

Descriptive Summary

  • Summarize findings based on frequency and trends (studies per year, geographic distribution, methodologies, research trends), use tables and figures, triangulate findings. ChatGPT prompt example provided.

Narrative Synthesis

  • Use Custom GPT or SciSpace to structure insights, group studies by themes, validate AI themes, identify patterns and inconsistencies, ensure synthesized insights align with full texts, document frameworks, figures, tables, page numbers.

Thematic & Visual Analysis

  • Thematic synthesis to map gaps, revisit criteria if themes unclear, address contradictions, use visualization tools (concept maps, tables, citation networks).

AI-Assisted Analysis & Synthesis

  • Use Custom GPT, Converse, or SciSpace to break down info, synthesize data, identify gaps, suggest directions, categorize studies, create summary tables.

Track & Document

  • Keep records of data organization, document thematic categorization decisions, ensure transparency with references.

Outputs of Step 5

  • Structured descriptive summary.
  • Thematic synthesis report.
  • Visual representations (tables, concept maps, citation networks).
  • Identified knowledge gaps.

2.6 Step 6: Interpret Results

Involves drawing meaningful insights while maintaining objectivity. Highlight trends, gaps, implications, not conclusions. AI generates summaries, proposes applications, flags inconsistencies, compares results. Human oversight assesses contextual accuracy, ensures coherence, validates implications, integrates theories, refines contradictions. Guidance: compare AI conclusions with original studies, apply models like PRISMA-ScR, discuss with experts, consider practical applications, validate gaps.

Translating Research into Action

Scoping reviews map knowledge and provide actionable insights for TIM. Identify opportunities for new product development, inform strategic technology adoption, develop competitive intelligence reports, identify partners. Templates for each provided.

Explain Significance & Identify Limitations

  • Relate findings to review question, discuss contribution, identify key takeaways. Acknowledge data gaps, publication bias, study diversity limitations.

AI-Assisted Interpretation & Maintain Broad Perspective

  • Over-reliance on AI for interpretation poses risks (accuracy, bias, lack of critical evaluation), AI should complement, not replace human expertise. Use AI tools (ChatGPT-4o, Jenni) to summarize trends, fact-check AI outputs, refine interpretations. Emphasize mapping evidence, not firm conclusions, focus on emerging directions, present multiple perspectives.

Outputs of Step 6

  • Synthesized discussion of key findings.
  • List of research gaps and future directions.
  • Limitations section.
  • Refined conclusions based on AI insights.

2.7 Step 7: Write the Scoping Review

AI generates summaries, intros, discussion sections, suggests structure. Human oversight refines AI text for coherence, ensures rigor, checks citations. Guidance: use AI for drafting, manual edits for clarity, validate citations.

Turn Findings into Valuable Assets & Templates

Transform findings into pitches, product development, strategic decisions, market research, competitive intelligence, partnerships, publications, theses, policies, training, grants. Templates provided for product development, technology adoption, competitive intelligence, partnerships.

Solicit Feedback from AI Tools & Experts

Feedback enhances rigor. AI tools (Gemini, Grok, OpenAI, Perplexity) provide structural/analytical feedback. Expert feedback (faculty, industry) gives contextual/strategic insights. Iterative refinement loop: draft, AI feedback, expert review, iterate, validate.

Structure Manuscript & AI-Assisted Writing

  • Follow PRISMA-ScR guidelines (Intro, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusion). Use ChatGPT-4o for initial drafts (manual refinement needed), Grammarly for readability/plagiarism, manual editing for clarity, fact-check AI content.

Revision, Quality Control, Report & Document

  • Multiple revision rounds, cross-check findings, ensure rigor, document methods, consider JBI guidelines, transparency on AI use.

Outputs of Step 7

  • Complete, structured manuscript.
  • Refined AI-assisted content.
  • Well-documented methodology.
  • Revised and proofread last version.

2.8 Step 8: Incorporate Ethical Considerations

Ethical integrity requires transparency in data, AI use, methodology. Disclose AI use, prevent biases, adhere to guidelines. AI analyzes literature for biases, highlights transparency lacks, recommends guidelines, scans ethics discussions. Human oversight evaluates AI biases, ensures responsible AI use, verifies ethical frameworks, checks integrity, assesses inclusivity. Guidance: engage ethics boards, follow policies, disclose AI contributions, seek diverse studies, ensure data transparency.

Ethical Implications of AI in TIM

  • Bias and fairness: AI models may inherit biases, leading to unequal access. Mitigation: Audit AI models, diverse datasets, human oversight.
  • Transparency and accountability: AI insights must be traceable. Mitigation: Explainable AI, documentation.
  • Data privacy and security: AI processes sensitive data. Mitigation: Cybersecurity, anonymization, legal compliance.
  • Impact on employment: Automation may displace roles. Mitigation: Upskilling professionals, AI as collaborator.

AI Transparency & Documentation

  • Disclose AI processes, document AI influence, validate AI content, include disclaimers. Prevent plagiarism with Turnitin, manual reviews, bias assessment. Mitigate bias by ensuring diverse perspectives. Ethical use of stakeholder input (approvals, confidentiality). Compliance with guidelines (institutional protocols, ACM Code of Ethics), avoid AI tools storing personal data, consider methodology registration, maintain audit trail. Data privacy compliance, avoid storing proprietary data, keep data handling records.

Outputs of Step 8

  • Transparent AI usage statement.
  • Plagiarism and bias report.
  • Documented audit trail of AI methods.
  • Compliance checklist.

Policy Briefs, Digital Platforms, Public Engagement
