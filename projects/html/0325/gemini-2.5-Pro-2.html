Guide to Produce Scoping Literature Reviews Using AI Tools

A step-by-step method for leveraging AI in scoping reviews, focusing on ethical, current, and clear practices for TIM students and faculty.

Objective: Empowering TIM Researchers

This guide aims to help TIM students and faculty:

  • Formulate clear and effective review questions.
  • Conduct thorough and reproducible scoping reviews.
  • Utilize AI tools ethically for study selection, data extraction, synthesis, and feedback.
  • Contribute to the guide's evolution and foster a collaborative learning community.

Part 1: Foundation of Scoping Reviews

Glossary

Boolean operators
Logical connectors (AND, OR, NOT) used to refine database searches.
Grey literature
Information outside traditional publishing (reports, conference proceedings, etc.).
Human oversight
Human judgment in monitoring and guiding automated systems for ethical and accurate outcomes.
PRISMA-ScR
Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses for Scoping Reviews guidelines.
Scoping review
Maps key concepts, evidence, and gaps in a field, typically without quality assessment.
Systematic review
Structured review answering a specific question using rigorous methods to synthesize studies.

A scoping review maps what's known and unknown in a field, inspiring business ideas or research. In Technology Innovation Management (TIM), they reveal insights into areas like blockchain, open innovation, or AI startups, highlighting opportunities. By combining human expertise with AI, TIM researchers can efficiently sift through diverse studies, uncover practical insights, and guide future innovation strategies. Unlike systematic reviews, scoping reviews cast a wider net, ideal for rapidly evolving tech landscapes.

1.1 When to Use a Scoping Review

Scoping reviews are valuable in TIM, especially when:

Broad/Exploratory Questions

Exploring a wide variety of studies rather than measuring effectiveness. E.g., How AI platforms transform tech commercialization.

Emerging Topics

Mapping new fields where concepts and gaps are unclear. E.g., Quantum computing applications in startups.

Heterogeneous Literature

Integrating diverse methodologies and disciplines where systematic synthesis is impractical. E.g., AI use across healthcare, finance, manufacturing.

Minimal Critical Appraisal

When assessing study quality isn't the primary goal. E.g., Cataloging open innovation strategies.

Identifying Gaps & Trends

Summarizing research, spotting gaps, and informing future directions. E.g., Uncharted areas in blockchain supply chains.

1.2 Key Frameworks

Scoping reviews in TIM often follow one or more frameworks:

Arksey and O’Malley (2005)

Five-step outline, adaptable for fast-changing fields like fintech. Good for broadly mapping literature (journals, patents, whitepapers).

Example: Mapping wearable tech for remote patient monitoring.

Levac et al. (2010)

Extends Arksey & O’Malley, stressing stakeholder engagement—vital for collaborating with startups, R&D teams.

Example: Engaging stakeholders for a review on IoT in smart manufacturing.

Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI) Framework

Refined methodology for planning and conducting reviews, ensuring rigor when examining diverse sources (patents, standards, market analyses).

Example: Using JBI for a review on 3D-printing for rapid prototyping.

PRISMA-ScR

Focuses on standardized reporting, making findings more credible and replicable.

Example: Following PRISMA-ScR for a review on blockchain in supply chains.

Recent Updates (2020–Present)

Incorporate AI tools to manage expanding literature volume, speeding up data collection and analysis.

Example: Using AI tools for a review on digital entrepreneurship post-COVID-19.

1.4 Limitations of Scoping Reviews

While useful, scoping reviews have drawbacks:

Lack of Critical Appraisal

May include low-quality studies, inflating perceived effectiveness.

Broad Scope

High-level overview may lack depth for specific recommendations.

No Strength-of-Evidence Assessment

Difficult to compare findings from diverse methodologies uniformly.

Selection Bias

Risk of overemphasizing cited clusters, missing newer areas.

Heterogeneity of Studies

Combining disparate data types complicates thematic analysis.

Lack of Meta-Analysis

Cannot calculate quantitative effect sizes (e.g., ROI).

Time/Resource Intensive

Mining complex domains requires extensive, iterative searching.

Difficulty Defining Scope

Balancing comprehensiveness and feasibility is tricky in fast-moving fields.

Risk of Overlooking Grey Literature

Missing industry reports or startup pitches can undervalue real-world insights.

Challenges in Updating

Rapid tech shifts demand frequent re-scoping to stay current.

1.5 Limitations of Using AI Tools

AI improves productivity but requires caution:

1.5.1 AI-created mistakes ('hallucinations')

Limitation: May invent references or misattribute authors.

Mitigation: Double-check citations; use reference managers (Zotero, Mendeley).

1.5.2 Lack of Contextual Understanding

Limitation: May oversimplify complex models or miss nuances.

Mitigation: Rely on human expertise; validate summaries with original studies/experts.

1.5.3 Bias in AI-Generated Content

Limitation: May reflect biases favoring Anglophone research or well-funded labs.

Mitigation: Conduct diverse searches; triangulate insights with multiple sources.

1.5.4 Overreliance on Recent Literature

Limitation: Might miss foundational works or early research.

Mitigation: Use manual backward citation; scan references in key papers.

1.5.5 Difficulty Managing Grey Literature

Limitation: Commonly overlooks whitepapers, policy briefs, VC reports.

Mitigation: Supplement with targeted grey literature searches; manually assess non-academic sources.

1.5.6 Inconsistent Handling of Methodologies

Limitation: May wrongly classify diverse study designs together.

Mitigation: Manually confirm study design; engage experts for methodological appraisal.

1.5.7 Ethical Concerns and Plagiarism Risks

Limitation: Generated text might resemble existing literature too closely.

Mitigation: Always cite sources; use plagiarism tools; follow ethical guidelines.

1.6 AI Biases

Be aware of biases that can shape your understanding:

Selection Bias

Bias: Overrepresentation of highly visible sources (e.g., leading journals), ignoring non-English or underrepresented regions.

Mitigation: Extend search to regional databases or local innovation hubs.

Algorithmic Bias

Bias: AI may amplify established perspectives (e.g., Silicon Valley success), downplaying alternatives.

Mitigation: Use multiple AI tools, cross-verify, highlight niche industries.

Confirmation Bias

Bias: AI aligns with initial search terms, potentially excluding contradictory findings.

Mitigation: Include counter-narratives; examine results critically.

Citation Bias

Bias: Prioritizes widely cited older work, ignoring newer influential preprints.

Mitigation: Update search parameters; include novel/disruptive studies.

Language & Accessibility Bias

Bias: Focuses on English-language open-access journals, omitting relevant studies in other languages.

Mitigation: Use multilingual databases; translate key non-English papers.

Data Hallucination

Bias: Might merge concepts, producing fictitious references.

Mitigation: Verify references in standard databases; investigate suspicious citations manually.

1.7 Human Oversight

In TIM scoping reviews, human oversight by researchers, faculty, or industry experts is crucial. It involves:

  • Monitoring AI outputs for domain accuracy (e.g., correct application of innovation frameworks).
  • Evaluating study suitability and credibility (e.g., ensuring relevant R&D projects are captured).
  • Intervening when AI misinterprets concepts (e.g., confusing digital entrepreneurship with only e-commerce).

Blend AI's efficiency with contextual knowledge of markets, investor behaviors, and regulations to maintain methodological soundness.

1.8 Step-by-step Approach to Grey Literature Searches

Grey literature offers real-world insights often missed by journals. Follow these steps:

  1. 1

    Define Scope

    Identify relevant reports, policy docs, whitepapers aligned with your question.

  2. 2

    Pick Keywords

    Outline core TIM concepts and synonyms; combine with Boolean operators.

  3. 3

    Select Sources

    • Government databases (policy frameworks, R&D funding).
    • Think tanks/NGOs (global tech adoption reports).
    • Industry reports (Gartner, Forrester, CB Insights, PitchBook).
    • Conference proceedings (IEEE, Academy of Management).
    • Institutional repositories (theses, working papers).
    • Patent databases (USPTO, Google Patents).
  4. 4

    Search Specialized Databases

    Use OpenGrey, NTIS, ProQuest Dissertations, Google Scholar (filtered).

  5. 5

    Search Competitor Information

    • List competitors.
    • Visit official websites (Newsroom, Investor Relations).
    • Monitor press releases (product launches, funding).
    • Check SEC filings (EDGAR for US).
    • Use specialized databases (Crunchbase, PitchBook).
    • Assess credibility and bias (cross-reference).
  6. 6

    Contact Organizations/Experts

    Reach out for unpublished data; network via LinkedIn/conferences.

  7. 7

    Filter and Evaluate Sources

    • Authority: Recognized source?
    • Credibility: Methods explained? Conflicts of interest?
    • Relevance: Addresses review question?
    • Timeliness: Still current?

Important Note on AI & Human Expertise

AI enhances efficiency but does not replace human expertise. Use AI for automation (summarization, categorization), but rely on human judgment for critical analysis, theoretical alignment, and decision-making. Manual oversight ensures accuracy and depth.

1. Formulate the Review Question and Scope

AI's Role

  • Generate question variations (e.g., via ChatGPT).
  • Refine clarity, suggest terms, related topics.
  • Analyze literature for gaps, key authors.

Human Oversight

  • Ensure clarity and feasibility (broad but structured).
  • Validate AI suggestions for focus and precision.
  • Incorporate relevant theoretical frameworks.

Guidance

  • Compare AI suggestions with published reviews.
  • Align question with frameworks like PCC.

2.1.2 PCC Framework for Structuring Questions

The Population, Concept, Context (PCC) framework ensures clarity:

Example Area Population Concept Context
Product Development Startups (AI products) Scaling challenges Venture acceleration
Digital Transformation Mid-sized manufacturers Industry 4.0 adoption N. American manufacturing
Quantum Security Cybersecurity firms, gov agencies Commercializing quantum-resistant tech National security/enterprise frameworks
Open Innovation Large tech firms Open innovation in R&D Global tech sector
Sustainability Hardware manufacturers Sustainable supply chains Global electronics industry

2.1.3 Alternative Frameworks

CIMO (Context, Intervention, Mechanism, Outcome)

Best for complex interventions. Ex: How AI software (I) enhances efficiency (O) in startups (C)?

ECLIPSE (Expectation, Client, Location, Impact, Professionals, Service)

For evaluating services. Ex: How frameworks (S) improved protection (I) for tech firms (C)?

PEO (Population, Exposure, Outcome)

Suitable for qualitative studies. Ex: Effects (O) of remote work (E) on engineers (P)?

SPIDER (Sample, Phenomenon, Design, Evaluation, Research type)

Useful for qualitative/mixed-methods. Ex: How managers (S) experience agile challenges (PI)?

2.1.4 Formulate the Scope

Define boundaries considering:

  • Breadth vs. Depth: Map emerging area vs. analyze sub-topic?
  • Time: Recent trends (e.g., quantum) vs. historical perspective (e.g., open innovation)?
  • Literature Type: Academic, industry reports, patents, empirical, conceptual?
  • Geography: Global vs. regional focus?
  • Stakeholders: Developers vs. end-users?

2.1.5 Outputs of Step 1

  • Clearly defined research question (using a framework).
  • Review objectives.
  • Preliminary keywords and search terms.
  • Selection of search strategies and AI tools.
  • Defined scope (inclusion/exclusion criteria, source types, disciplines).

2. Search for Articles

A thorough, iterative, and transparent search is essential.

AI's Role

  • Generate keyword variations & Boolean strings.
  • Identify relevant databases/sources.

Human Oversight

  • Refine AI queries (avoid irrelevant/missing studies).
  • Select appropriate databases (include grey lit).
  • Adjust Boolean operators manually for precision.

Guidance

  • Assess AI queries in multiple databases.
  • Validate keywords against prior reviews.
  • Ensure grey literature sources are included.

2.2.2 Develop Search Strategy

  • Define key terms, synonyms, and Boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT). Use AI (e.g., ChatGPT) for suggestions.
  • Adapt strategies for different databases (Scopus, IEEE Xplore, etc.).
  • Define inclusion/exclusion criteria for filtering later.

2.2.3 Recommended AI Tools for Searching

ChatGPT

Generate/refine terms, Boolean strings, summarize themes.

Consensus

Assess relevance, rank papers by consensus, check agreement.

Perplexity

Retrieve from academic/non-academic sources, find connections, latest research.

Elicit

Extract key insights, prioritize studies, compare findings.

OpenRead

Retrieve based on meaning, extract relevant parts, summarize complex papers.

2.2.4/2.2.5 Sources & Databases

Leverage diverse sources for a well-rounded review. Use multiple databases for comprehensive coverage:

Key Sources:
  • Academic Databases (Scopus, Web of Science, IEEE Xplore)
  • Industry Reports (Gartner, McKinsey, CB Insights)
  • Preprints/White Papers (ArXiv, SSRN)
  • Patent Databases (Google Patents, WIPO)
  • Grey Literature Repositories (OpenGrey, OAIster)
Database Types:
  • Business/Tech (IEEE Xplore, ABI/INFORM)
  • Multidisciplinary (Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar)
  • Social Sciences (PsycINFO, Sociological Abstracts)
  • Health Sciences (PubMed, CINAHL)

2.2.6 Example Search Queries

// Digital transformation in SMEs
("digital transformation" OR "Industry 4.0" OR "AI adoption") AND ("small and medium enterprises" OR "SMEs") AND ("barriers" OR "challenges" OR "opportunities")
// Filters: Peer-reviewed, last 5 years, English
// Blockchain for supply chain transparency
("blockchain technology" OR "distributed ledger") AND ("supply chain management" OR "logistics") AND ("transparency" OR "traceability")
// Filters: Empirical/case studies, last 10 years, global

2.2.7 Manage & Document

  • Use reference managers (Zotero, Mendeley) to organize/deduplicate.
  • Track search strategies (databases, terms, results) for reproducibility.
  • Refine searches iteratively based on findings.
  • Document the process using a PRISMA-ScR Flow Diagram.
  • Report search dates, limits, and selection justifications.

2.2.9 Outputs of Step 2

  • Documented search strategy (terms, databases, dates).
  • Collection of relevant articles in a reference manager.
  • PRISMA-ScR flow diagram draft.
  • Refined search query.
  • List of excluded articles (with reasons, if screened here).

3. Select Articles

A structured, transparent selection process using two-phase screening.

AI's Role

  • Classify/rank articles by relevance.
  • Summarize abstracts for quick evaluation.
  • Detect duplicates.

Human Oversight

  • Review inclusion/exclusion criteria manually (AI can misclassify).
  • Assess study quality/relevance critically.
  • Identify hidden biases (e.g., favoring recent studies).

Guidance

  • Conduct manual spot-checks (verify AI exclusions).
  • Ensure key studies aren't missed.
  • Use dual human screening for accuracy.

2.3.2 Screening Strategy

Phase 1: Title/Abstract Screening

Remove clearly irrelevant studies. Use tools like Rayyan AI for rapid filtering. Document criteria changes.

Phase 2: Full-Text Screening

Assess remaining articles against inclusion criteria. Justify and log exclusions.

2.3.3 Using Rayyan AI & Dual Screening

Rayyan AI Features:
  • Import references (RIS, BibTeX).
  • Define inclusion/exclusion criteria.
  • Enable collaborative screening (multiple reviewers).
  • AI-powered prioritization (suggests relevant studies).
  • Tagging & categorization (e.g., "high relevance", "exclude").
  • Blind review mode to reduce bias.
Importance of Dual Screening

Two independent reviewers assess each study against criteria. This minimizes bias, enhances reliability, ensures consistency, and increases credibility, especially for complex TIM topics. Discrepancies are resolved through discussion or a third reviewer.

2.3.4 Track and Document

  • Maintain and update the PRISMA-ScR flow diagram.
  • Keep a record of excluded articles with reasons.

2.3.5 Outputs of Step 3

  • Refined set of eligible articles.
  • Completed PRISMA-ScR flow diagram.
  • List of excluded articles with reasons.
  • Potentially updated inclusion/exclusion criteria.
  • Records of title/abstract and full-text screening results.

4. Extract Data

Systematically capture relevant information from selected studies. AI assists, but manual oversight is key.

AI's Role

  • Extract key themes, concepts, study details.
  • Summarize findings across papers.
  • Group studies into categories.

Human Oversight

  • Verify extracted info (AI can misinterpret tables/figures).
  • Check contextual accuracy (AI summaries may lack nuance).
  • Ensure methodological consistency.

Guidance

  • Manually extract data from a sample for comparison.
  • Use a structured data extraction form (Excel, Covidence).
  • Ensure AI categories align with the review question.

2.4.2 Extract Key Information

Recommended AI tools:

  • ChatGPT 4.0: Basic textual summaries.
  • Elicit, SciSpace: Extract data into tables (add custom columns in Elicit).
  • Research Rabbit, Litmaps, VosViewer: Visualize citation networks.

Key data elements:

  • Citation details, Study type/methodology, Population/sample
  • Key concepts/themes, Main findings/contributions, Knowledge gaps

5. Analyze and Synthesize Data

Organize, summarize, and present findings meaningfully, focusing on mapping literature and thematic grouping.

AI's Role

  • Summarize key themes.
  • Categorize studies.
  • Generate preliminary thematic maps.
  • Identify patterns.

Human Oversight

  • Verify thematic accuracy (AI themes can be broad/miss links).
  • Ensure methodological consistency (don't mix designs inappropriately).
  • Assess depth and relevance (AI might focus on word frequency over concepts).
  • Refine AI categories based on research objectives/theory.
  • Validate research gaps against expert knowledge.

Guidance

  • Read original studies sample to confirm AI findings.
  • Organize themes/characteristics manually for quality control.
  • Start with AI categories, then refine with expert input.
  • Align findings with established theoretical models.
  • Discuss synthesis with experts/colleagues.

2.5.2 Descriptive Summary

  • Summarize findings by frequency/trends (studies per year, geography, methods).
  • Use tables/figures for visual representation.
  • Triangulate findings from different sources.
  • Example Prompt (ChatGPT): "Summarize key findings of [X articles] on [topic] and identify common themes/challenges."

2.5.3 Narrative Synthesis

  • Use AI (Custom GPT, SciSpace) to help structure insights.
  • Group studies into conceptual categories based on themes.
  • Manually validate AI-generated themes before integration.
  • Identify patterns, relationships, inconsistencies across studies.
  • Document supporting frameworks, figures, tables, page numbers.

2.5.4 Thematic and Visual Analysis

  • Conduct thematic synthesis to map gaps and clusters.
  • Revisit criteria or extraction if themes are unclear.
  • Use visualization tools (concept maps, tables, citation networks).

2.5.7 Outputs of Step 5

  • Structured descriptive summary (study characteristics, trends).
  • Thematic synthesis report (narrative grouping studies).
  • Visual representations (tables, maps, networks).
  • Identified knowledge gaps.

6. Interpret Results

Draw meaningful insights, highlighting trends, gaps, and implications, while maintaining a broad perspective.

AI's Role

  • Generate summaries of findings, major themes.
  • Propose applications, recommendations, future research.
  • Flag inconsistencies, underexplored topics.
  • Compare results, suggest explanations for variations.

Human Oversight

  • Assess contextual accuracy (AI may miss nuances).
  • Ensure logical coherence (AI might draw frequency-based conclusions).
  • Validate AI-suggested implications (AI lacks real-world expertise).
  • Integrate theoretical frameworks.
  • Refine interpretations of contradictions/limitations.

Guidance

  • Compare AI conclusions with original studies.
  • Apply established models (PRISMA-ScR, thematic analysis).
  • Discuss AI interpretations with experts/colleagues.
  • Ensure interpretations consider practical applications.
  • Validate gaps against prior literature.

2.6.3 Explain Significance & Identify Limitations

  • Relate findings clearly to review question/objectives.
  • Discuss contributions to the broader field.
  • Identify key takeaways for practice, policy, research.
  • Acknowledge data gaps, publication bias, study diversity limitations.
2.6.5 Caution: Overreliance on AI Interpretation

AI tools assist, but over-reliance risks inaccuracy, bias, hallucinations, and lack of critical evaluation. AI should complement, not replace, human judgment. Always verify AI insights, cross-check sources, and apply domain expertise.

Use AI (ChatGPT-4o, Jenni) to assist, but fact-check, ensure alignment with evidence, and avoid bias.

2.6.7 Outputs of Step 6

  • Synthesized discussion of key findings.
  • List of research gaps and future directions.
  • Limitations section (data, bias, methodology).
  • Refined, validated conclusions.

7. Write the Scoping Review

AI's Role

  • Check for citation errors (hallucinations, formatting).

Guidance

  • Use AI for drafting, not finalization.
  • Apply manual edits for clarity and academic style.
  • Validate all citations using trusted sources.

8. Incorporate Ethical Considerations

Ensure integrity through transparency in data handling, AI usage, and documentation.

AI's Role

  • Analyze literature for language patterns/biases.
  • Highlight studies lacking transparency/potential conflicts.
  • Recommend ethical guidelines.
  • Scan for discussions on ethical implications.

Human Oversight

  • Evaluate AI-identified biases (human judgment needed).
  • Ensure responsible AI use (accuracy, integrity).
  • Verify AI-suggested ethical frameworks (AI lacks moral reasoning).
  • Check for integrity issues (funding, conflicts).
  • Assess inclusivity and fairness.

Guidance

  • Engage ethical review boards if needed.
  • Follow institutional/journal/funder ethics policies.
  • Disclose AI contributions, ensure human oversight in interpretation.
  • Seek studies from diverse perspectives.
  • Ensure transparency (data collection, consent, funding).

2.8.2 Ethical Implications of AI in TIM

Consider the broader ethical landscape:

Bias & Fairness

AI models may inherit biases. Mitigation: Auditing, diverse data, human oversight.

Transparency & Accountability

AI insights must be interpretable. Mitigation: Explainable AI (XAI), clear documentation.

Data Privacy & Security

Risk of breaches, misuse of IP. Mitigation: Cybersecurity, anonymization, compliance (GDPR, CCPA).

Impact on Employment & Expertise

Automation may displace roles; over-reliance risks critical thinking. Mitigation: Upskilling, AI as a collaborator.

2.8.3 Key Ethical Practices

  • AI Transparency: Disclose AI use in methods, validate outputs, provide disclaimers.
  • Prevent Plagiarism: Cross-check AI content (Turnitin), review manually for misinformation.
  • Mitigate Bias: Ensure balanced representation of diverse perspectives.
  • Stakeholder Input: Get ethical approvals if needed, maintain confidentiality.
  • Compliance: Follow institutional/professional guidelines (e.g., ACM Code of Ethics), consider registering protocol (OSF, PROSPERO), maintain audit trail.
  • Data Privacy: Comply with regulations, avoid tools storing sensitive data.

2.8.9 Outputs of Step 8

  • Transparent AI usage statement.
  • Plagiarism and bias report/assessment.
  • Documented audit trail of AI methods.
  • Compliance checklist (ethics guidelines, policies).

9. Disseminate Findings

Ensure findings reach appropriate audiences through multiple strategies.

AI's Role

  • Generate summaries, infographics, slide decks.
  • Recommend suitable journals/conferences.
  • Simplify complex findings for broader audiences.
  • Help structure content for web/social media.

Human Oversight

  • Ensure accuracy in AI summaries (avoid overgeneralization).
  • Verify AI recommendations (relevance, credibility).
  • Maintain academic integrity (cite properly, disclose AI role).
  • Tailor AI outputs to target audience/format.
  • Ensure ethical and inclusive communication.

Guidance

  • Use AI as a starting point, not final draft.
  • Ensure recommendations align with requirements.
  • Publish via multiple channels (journals, conferences, repositories).

2.10 Checklist for Authors and Reviewers

Use this checklist to ensure a rigorous and high-quality TIM scoping review:

  • 1. Formulate Question & Scope: Clearly define PCC? Question specific & relevant? Scope balanced (breadth/depth, time, lit types, geo, stakeholders)?
  • 2. Identify & Select Studies: Diverse sources identified (academic, industry, patents)? Precise search strategy (Boolean, filters)? AI tools used for refinement?
  • 3. Screen & Select (Rayyan AI): References uploaded? Inclusion/exclusion applied? Dual screening conducted? AI assistance used appropriately? Studies tagged/categorized?
  • 4. Extract & Synthesize Data: Key characteristics extracted? Themes (drivers, barriers, factors) identified? Gaps recognized?
  • 5. Visualize & Report Insights: Concept maps/visualizations used? AI summaries leveraged appropriately? Network analysis applied if relevant?
  • 6. Translate Research to Action: Opportunities identified (product dev, strategy)? Competitive intelligence developed? Partners identified?
  • 7. Disseminate Findings: Vignette/pitch developed if applicable? Findings communicated effectively for impact?
  • 8. Ethical Considerations: Bias mitigation addressed? Transparency/accountability in AI use? Data privacy protected? AI balanced with human expertise?
