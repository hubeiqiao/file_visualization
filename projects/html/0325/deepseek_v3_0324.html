Part 1: Foundation of Scoping Reviews

Key Concepts

Scoping Review

A type of literature review that maps key concepts, evidence, and research gaps in a field, typically without assessing study quality.

Systematic Review

A structured and comprehensive review of existing research on a specific question, using rigorous methodology to identify and synthesize relevant studies.

When to Use Scoping Reviews in TIM

Broad or Exploratory Questions

When investigating a wide variety of studies rather than measuring effectiveness.

Emerging Topics

When key concepts, definitions, and knowledge gaps are unclear in new fields.

Heterogeneous Literature

When studies cover diverse methodologies making systematic synthesis impractical.

Identifying Gaps

When the goal is to summarize research and identify future directions rather than draw conclusions.

Limitations & Challenges

Scoping Review Limitations

  • Lack of critical appraisal
  • Broad and less focused scope
  • No formal strength-of-evidence assessment
  • Selection bias risks
  • Heterogeneity challenges

AI Tool Limitations

  • AI-created citation mistakes ("hallucinations")
  • Lack of contextual understanding
  • Bias in generated content
  • Overreliance on recent literature
  • Difficulty managing grey literature

Part 2: Method to Produce Scoping Reviews

9-Step Methodology

1. Formulate Question

Define research question and scope using AI-assisted refinement

2. Search Articles

Use AI-driven search strategies to retrieve relevant literature

3. Select Articles

Screen and filter studies using AI-assisted tools

4. Extract Data

Utilize AI to extract and organize key study insights

5. Analyze Data

Identify trends, themes, and gaps using AI-powered categorization

6. Interpret Results

Balance AI-generated insights with critical human evaluation

7. Write Review

Use AI for drafting while maintaining human oversight

8. Ethical Considerations

Apply ethical guidelines to AI integration and research integrity

9. Disseminate

Leverage AI for summarization and visualization

Key Methodological Components

Formulating Review Questions

Use frameworks like PCC (Population, Concept, Context) to structure questions:

Example:

"What are the key challenges influencing the scaling of AI-driven product prototypes in technology startups within venture acceleration programs?"

Search Strategies

Recommended AI tools for efficient searching:

  • ChatGPT for Boolean query generation
  • Consensus for relevance assessment
  • Perplexity for diverse source discovery

Article Selection

Use Rayyan AI for screening:

  • Upload references from databases
  • Apply inclusion/exclusion criteria
  • Conduct dual screening for accuracy

Data Extraction

Tools for organizing findings:

  • Elicit for custom column extraction
  • SciSpace for tabular data organization
  • Zotero for reference management

Visualizing Insights

Concept Maps

Visualize relationships between key themes

Citation Networks

Analyze connections between research papers

Trend Analysis

Track emerging patterns in literature

Ethical Considerations

Key Concerns

  • AI-generated citation errors
  • Algorithmic bias in search results
  • Data privacy and security
  • Transparency in AI usage

Mitigation Strategies

  • Manual verification of citations
  • Diverse database searches
  • Clear documentation of AI contributions
  • Human oversight at all stages
