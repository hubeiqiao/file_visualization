PART 1: Foundation of Scoping Reviews

This section lays the groundwork, defining scoping reviews, comparing them with systematic reviews, outlining key frameworks, and discussing the role, benefits, and limitations of AI in the Technology Innovation Management (TIM) context.

Glossary

Boolean operators
Logical connectors (AND, OR, NOT) for refining database searches.
Grey literature
Information outside traditional publishing (reports, conference proceedings).
Human oversight
Human judgment monitoring/guiding automated systems for ethical outcomes.
PRISMA-ScR
Guidelines for conducting and reporting scoping reviews.
Scoping review
Maps key concepts, evidence, and research gaps in a field.
Systematic review
Structured review answering a specific question, assessing study quality.

1.1 When to Use a Scoping Review

Ideal for TIM when exploring broad, emerging topics with diverse literature, especially when critical appraisal isn't the main goal.

  • Broad/exploratory questions (e.g., AI transforming commercialization).
  • Emerging topics (e.g., quantum computing in startups).
  • Heterogeneous literature (e.g., multidisciplinary AI use).
  • Minimal need for critical appraisal (e.g., cataloging open innovation strategies).
  • Identifying gaps and trends (e.g., blockchain in supply chains).

1.2 Key Frameworks

Arksey & O’Malley (2005)

Iterative 5-step outline, good for fast-changing fields (e.g., mapping fintech).

Levac et al. (2010)

Extends Arksey, stresses stakeholder engagement (e.g., engaging accelerators for IoT review).

JBI Framework

Refined methodology for rigor & transparency (e.g., 3D printing for prototyping review).

PRISMA-ScR

Standardized reporting for credibility (e.g., blockchain in supply chain reporting).

Recent Updates (2020+)

Incorporate AI tools for managing large volumes (e.g., AI screening for post-COVID digital entrepreneurship).

1.3 Benefits of Using AI

  • Speed & Efficiency: Rapid screening, automated deduplication.
  • Enhanced Discovery: Diverse sources, intelligent recommendations.
  • Improved Organization: Topic clustering, auto-tagging.
  • Scalable Analysis: Big data handling, dynamic updating.
  • Reduced Human Error: Consistent screening, model refinement.
  • Insight Generation: Text summarization, trend detection.
  • Cost Savings: Streamlined workflows, better resource allocation.

1.4 Limitations of Scoping Reviews

  • Lack of critical appraisal (may include low-quality studies).
  • Broad scope can lack depth for specific recommendations.
  • No formal strength-of-evidence assessment.
  • Risk of selection bias without strict criteria.
  • Heterogeneity of studies complicates synthesis.
  • No meta-analysis (no calculated effect sizes).
  • Can be time and resource-intensive.
  • Difficulty defining scope in fast-moving fields.
  • Risk of overlooking valuable grey literature.
  • Challenges in keeping reviews updated.

1.5 Limitations of Using AI Tools

AI 'Hallucinations'

AI might invent citations. Mitigation: Double-check all references; use reference managers.

Lack of Context

AI may oversimplify complex models. Mitigation: Rely on human expertise; validate with original studies.

Bias in Content

AI may reflect existing biases (e.g., geographic, funding). Mitigation: Diverse database searches; triangulate sources.

Overreliance on Recent

AI might miss foundational work. Mitigation: Manual backward citation chasing; scan key papers' references.

Difficulty with Grey Lit

AI often overlooks reports, whitepapers. Mitigation: Targeted grey lit searches; manual assessment.

Inconsistent Methodology Handling

AI may misclassify study types. Mitigation: Manual confirmation; expert appraisal.

Ethical/Plagiarism Risks

AI text might resemble existing work. Mitigation: Always cite; use plagiarism checkers; follow guidelines.

1.6 AI Biases & Mitigation

Selection Bias

Issue: Overrepresents visible sources (e.g., top journals), ignores others (non-English, regional).
Example: Prioritizing Silicon Valley reports, ignoring SE Asia case studies.
Mitigation: Search regional databases, local innovation hubs.

Algorithmic Bias

Issue: Amplifies established views, downplays alternatives.
Example: AI trained on software data misclassifies hardware innovation as irrelevant.
Mitigation: Use multiple AI tools, cross-verify, highlight niche areas.

Confirmation Bias

Issue: Aligns with initial terms (e.g., "successful"), excludes contradictions.
Example: Filtering for "successful blockchain pilots" excludes failed ones, inflating success perception.
Mitigation: Include counter-narratives (e.g., "failures"); critical examination.

Citation Bias

Issue: Prioritizes highly cited older work, ignores newer influential preprints.
Example: AI ranks by citation count, missing groundbreaking but less-cited early research on green tech.
Mitigation: Update parameters; include novel/disruptive studies.

Language & Accessibility Bias

Issue: Focuses on English open-access, omits other languages.
Example: AI searches English-only DBs, missing non-English open innovation case studies.
Mitigation: Use multilingual databases; translate key papers.

Data Hallucination

Issue: AI merges concepts, creates fictitious references.
Example: AI invents a non-existent conference paper by merging details from multiple sources.
Mitigation: Verify references in standard databases; investigate suspicious citations manually.

1.7 Human Oversight

Essential for ensuring accuracy, relevance, and ethical integrity. Involves experts monitoring AI outputs, evaluating study suitability, and intervening when AI misinterprets concepts. Blends AI efficiency with human contextual knowledge.

1.8 Grey Literature Searches

Crucial for real-world TIM insights often missed by traditional journals.

  1. Define scope (relevant reports, policy docs).
  2. Outline keywords & Boolean operators.
  3. Select sources (Gov databases, think tanks, industry reports - Gartner/CB Insights, patent DBs, conferences, institutional repos).
  4. Search specialized databases (OpenGrey, NTIS, ProQuest, Google Scholar filters).
  5. Search competitor info (websites, press releases, SEC filings, Crunchbase).
  6. Contact organizations & experts directly.
  7. Filter & evaluate sources (Authority, Credibility, Relevance, Timeliness).

1.9 Reference Management

Essential for organizing hundreds of diverse TIM sources.

Zotero (Recommended)

  • Open source, great web capture.
  • Google Docs integration.
  • Integrates with AI plugins (ARIA, ResearchRabbit).

Mendeley

  • AI-driven paper suggestions.
  • Strong PDF annotation & social features.

EndNote

  • Ideal for large-scale data management.
  • Integrates with external AI tools.

2.1 Step 1: Formulate the Review Question and Scope

Define a broad, exploratory question using frameworks like PCC. AI assists in generating variations and identifying gaps, but human oversight ensures clarity and theoretical alignment.

AI Role & Human Oversight

AI: Generate question variations (ChatGPT prompt example provided), suggest terms, highlight gaps.

Human: Ensure clarity/feasibility, validate AI suggestions, incorporate theoretical frameworks.

Guidance: Compare AI suggestions with published reviews, align with frameworks (PCC).

PCC Framework Examples

Alternative frameworks: CIMO, ECLIPSE, PEO, SPIDER.

Outputs: Defined question, objectives, keyword list, search strategies, scope definition (inclusion/exclusion, sources, disciplines).

2.2 Step 2: Search for Articles

Conduct a thorough, iterative search using AI to generate keywords/strings, but rely on human oversight to refine queries, select databases (incl. grey lit), and adjust operators.

AI Role & Human Oversight

AI: Generate keyword variations, Boolean strings, suggest databases.

Human: Refine queries, select appropriate databases (incl. grey lit), adjust Boolean operators.

Guidance: Test queries in multiple DBs, validate keywords, ensure grey lit inclusion.

Recommended AI Tools

ChatGPT (Keywords, Summaries)
Consensus (Relevance, Agreement)
Perplexity (Broad Retrieval, Connections)
Elicit (Insight Extraction, Prioritization)
OpenRead (Meaning-based Retrieval, Summaries)

Sources & Databases

Use academic DBs (Scopus, IEEE Xplore), industry reports (Gartner), preprints (arXiv), patent DBs (Google Patents), and grey lit sources (OpenGrey).

Example Search Query (Blockchain for Supply Chain)

("blockchain technology" OR "distributed ledger" OR "smart contracts") AND ("supply chain management" OR "logistics" OR "procurement") AND ("transparency" OR "traceability" OR "fraud prevention")

Outputs: Documented search strategy, collection of articles in reference manager, PRISMA-ScR flow diagram, refined query, list of excluded articles.

2.3 Step 3: Select Articles

Use a two-phase screening (Title/Abstract, Full-Text). AI (e.g., Rayyan) assists classification and duplicate detection, but human oversight validates criteria application and assesses relevance. Dual screening is crucial.

AI Role & Human Oversight

AI: Classify/rank articles, summarize abstracts, detect duplicates (e.g., Rayyan).

Human: Review inclusion/exclusion criteria manually, assess study quality/relevance, identify hidden biases.

Guidance: Manual spot-checks, ensure key studies aren't missed, use dual human screening.

Screening Strategy


1. Title/Abstract Screening (Rayyan AI assists)

2. Full-Text Screening (Assess alignment)

Using Rayyan AI

  • Upload references (RIS/BibTeX).
  • Set criteria, enable collaborative screening.
  • Use AI prioritization, tagging, blind review mode.
  • Emphasize dual screening for minimizing bias and enhancing reliability.

Outputs: Refined set of eligible articles, updated PRISMA-ScR diagram, list of excluded articles with reasons, updated criteria.

2.4 Step 4: Extract Data

Systematically capture key info. AI (Elicit, SciSpace) helps extract themes and summarize, but human oversight verifies accuracy, context, and methodological consistency.

AI Role & Human Oversight

AI: Extract themes/concepts, summarize findings, group studies (Elicit, SciSpace, ChatGPT).

Human: Verify extracted info (tables, figures), check contextual accuracy, ensure methodological consistency.

Guidance: Manually extract a sample for comparison, use structured extraction forms, ensure categories align with review question.

Key Data Elements & AI Tools

Extract: Citation, Study type, Population, Concepts, Findings, Gaps. Adjust categories iteratively.

Tools: Elicit/SciSpace for table extraction; ChatGPT for summaries; Research Rabbit/Litmaps/VOSviewer for citation networks.

Example Data Extraction Table

Visualize insights using concept maps (CmapTools, MindMeister), AI summaries, and citation networks.

Ensure data quality via manual verification and ethical review (JBI guidelines, bias assessment if needed).

Outputs: Completed extraction tables, structured dataset, refined summaries, final extraction template, records of adjustments.

2.5 Step 5: Analyze and Synthesize Data

Organize and summarize findings thematically. AI assists with summarization and categorization, but human oversight verifies accuracy, ensures consistency, assesses relevance, and aligns with theory.

AI Role & Human Oversight

AI: Summarize themes, categorize studies, generate preliminary maps, identify patterns.

Human: Verify thematic accuracy, ensure methodological consistency, assess depth/relevance, refine categories, validate gaps.

Guidance: Read original studies, organize manually for quality control, refine AI categories, align with theory, discuss with experts.

Analysis Approaches

  • Descriptive Summary: Summarize frequencies, trends (studies/year, geography, methods). Use tables/figures. Triangulate findings.
  • Narrative Synthesis: Group studies conceptually (Custom GPT, SciSpace assist). Validate themes manually. Identify patterns/inconsistencies.
  • Thematic & Visual Analysis: Map knowledge gaps/clusters. Use concept maps, tables, citation networks.

Track and document analysis decisions and outputs.

Outputs: Structured descriptive summary, thematic synthesis report, visual representations, identified knowledge gaps.

2.6 Step 6: Interpret Results

Draw meaningful insights, highlighting trends, gaps, and implications. AI can suggest summaries and applications, but human oversight assesses context, coherence, validates implications, and integrates theory.

AI Role & Human Oversight

AI: Summarize findings, propose applications/recommendations, flag inconsistencies.

Human: Assess contextual accuracy, ensure logical coherence, validate implications, integrate theory, refine contradictions.

Guidance: Compare AI conclusions with studies, apply frameworks, discuss with experts, consider practical applications, validate gaps.

Translating Research into Action

Use findings to:

  • Identify new product opportunities.
  • Inform technology adoption decisions.
  • Develop competitive intelligence reports.
  • Identify potential partners/collaborators.

Explain significance, identify limitations (data gaps, bias, diversity), use AI cautiously (verify insights), maintain broad perspective.

Outputs: Synthesized discussion, list of research gaps/future directions, limitations section, refined conclusions.

2.7 Step 7: Write the Scoping Review

Structure the manuscript following PRISMA-ScR. AI assists drafting, but human oversight refines text, ensures rigor, checks citations, and turns findings into valuable assets.

AI Role & Human Oversight

AI: Generate drafts (summaries, intro, discussion), suggest structure (ChatGPT, Grammarly).

Human: Refine AI text for coherence/rigor, ensure academic standards, check citations.

Guidance: Use AI for drafting, not final text. Manual edits for clarity/tone. Validate all citations.

Turn Findings into Assets

Transform review findings into:

  • Venture pitches
  • Product opportunities
  • Strategic decisions
  • Market research
  • Competitive intelligence
  • Partnership identification
  • Journal publications
  • TIM theses/projects
  • Policy recommendations
  • Training materials
  • Grant proposals

Templates & Feedback

Use provided templates for product dev, tech adoption, competitive intel, and partnerships.

Solicit feedback from AI tools (Gemini, Grok, Perplexity Deep Research) for structure/analysis and human experts for context/strategy. Iterate based on feedback.

Structure & Writing

Follow PRISMA-ScR structure (Intro, Methods, Results, Discussion, Conclusion). Use AI writing assistants cautiously, fact-check, manually edit, perform quality control.

Outputs: Complete manuscript, refined AI-assisted content, documented methodology, revised final version.

2.8 Step 8: Incorporate Ethical Considerations

Ensure integrity through transparency in AI use, bias prevention, and adherence to guidelines. AI can analyze bias patterns, but human oversight evaluates them, ensures responsible AI use, verifies ethical frameworks, and checks integrity.

AI Role & Human Oversight

AI: Analyze literature for bias patterns, flag transparency issues, suggest guidelines.

Human: Evaluate AI-identified biases, ensure responsible AI use (accuracy, integrity), verify ethical frameworks, check for integrity issues (funding, conflicts), assess inclusivity.

Guidance: Engage review boards, follow policies, disclose AI use, seek diverse studies, ensure transparency.

Key Ethical Areas

  • AI Implications in TIM: Address bias/fairness, transparency/accountability, data privacy/security, impact on employment/expertise.
  • AI Transparency & Documentation: Clearly disclose AI use, document processes, validate outputs.
  • Plagiarism Prevention: Cross-check AI content (Turnitin), review manually.
  • Bias Mitigation: Ensure balanced representation.
  • Ethical Stakeholder Input: Get approvals, maintain confidentiality.
  • Compliance: Follow institutional/professional codes (e.g., ACM Code), consider OSF/PROSPERO registration, maintain audit trail.
  • Data Privacy: Comply with regulations, avoid insecure tools.

Outputs: Transparent AI usage statement, plagiarism/bias report, documented audit trail, compliance checklist.

2.9 Step 9: Disseminate Findings

Share results effectively with relevant audiences. AI helps generate summaries/visuals and suggest outlets, but human oversight ensures accuracy, verifies recommendations, maintains integrity, and tailors strategies.

AI Role & Human Oversight

AI: Generate summaries, infographics, slide decks; recommend journals/conferences; simplify findings.

Human: Ensure accuracy, verify recommendations, maintain integrity, tailor strategies, ensure ethical communication.

Guidance: Use AI as starting point, verify suggestions, publish widely, translate for practice, disclose AI use.

Dissemination Channels

Research Vignette: Concise summary for decision-makers (Title, Background, Findings, Impact, Next Steps).
Pitch Presentation: For ventures/tech (Problem, Market, Solution, Competition, Business Model, Roadmap, Funding).
Peer-Reviewed Journals: Academic publication (consider open access).
Conference Presentations: Engage community (oral, poster, panel).
Policy Briefs/Reports: Translate for policymakers/practitioners.
Digital/Open Science: Preprints (arXiv), repositories (OSF), registries (PROSPERO), datasets (Zenodo).
Public Engagement: Blogs, podcasts, social media, media outreach, infographics.

Outputs: Journal article, conference presentation, policy brief, open-access materials, engagement materials.

2.10 Checklist for Authors and Reviewers

  • Define clear question & scope (PCC).
  • Identify & select relevant studies (diverse sources, clear strategy, AI assist).
  • Screen using Rayyan (criteria, dual screening, AI assist).
  • Extract & synthesize data systematically.
  • Visualize & report insights clearly (maps, summaries, networks).
  • Translate research into actionable outputs (products, strategy, intel, partners).
  • Disseminate findings effectively (vignette, pitch, communication).
  • Address ethical considerations (bias, transparency, privacy, human oversight).
