Guide to Producing Scoping Literature Reviews Using AI Tools

A step-by-step method for TIM students and faculty to leverage AI for ethical, efficient, and impactful scoping reviews.

Part 1: Foundation of Scoping Reviews

This section lays the groundwork, defining scoping reviews, explaining when to use them, introducing key frameworks, and discussing the role of AI, its benefits, limitations, and ethical considerations.

Glossary

Boolean operators: Logical connectors (AND, OR, NOT) used in database searches to refine results.
Grey literature: Information produced outside traditional channels (e.g., reports, conference proceedings).
Human oversight: Human judgment monitoring AI systems for ethical and accurate outcomes.
PRISMA-ScR: Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses for Scoping Reviews guidelines.
Scoping review: Maps key concepts, evidence, and gaps in a field, typically without quality assessment.
Systematic review: Structured review answering a specific question using rigorous methods to appraise and synthesize studies.

Introduction

A scoping review gathers and examines a wide range of sources to show what is known and unknown, helping identify gaps for new business ideas or research questions. In Technology Innovation Management (TIM), scoping reviews are crucial for informing:

Venture Pitches
Market Analysis
Business Opportunities
Product Development
Process Improvement
Competitive Landscape

Business Models

Innovation Ecosystems
Research Vignettes
TIM Projects/Theses

By combining human expertise with AI tools, TIM researchers can efficiently sift through vast literature, uncover insights from diverse sources, and guide future innovation strategies. Unlike systematic reviews, scoping reviews cast a wider net, ideal for rapidly evolving technology landscapes.

If your question is narrow, aims to evaluate intervention quality, or measures effectiveness, a systematic review is more suitable.

When to Use a Scoping Review vs. Systematic Review

In TIM, scoping reviews are particularly valuable when:

Scoping Review Is Ideal For:

  • Broad/Exploratory Questions: Exploring a wide variety of studies on a topic. Example: How AI platforms transform tech commercialization.
  • Emerging Topics: Mapping new fields where concepts and gaps are unclear. Example: Quantum computing in early-stage startups.
  • Heterogeneous Literature: Integrating diverse methodologies and disciplines. Example: AI use across healthcare, finance, manufacturing.
  • Minimal Critical Appraisal Need: When assessing study quality is not the primary goal. Example: Cataloging open innovation strategies.
  • Identifying Gaps & Trends: Summarizing research and informing future directions. Example: Spotting gaps in blockchain supply chain management.

Systematic Review Is Better For:

  • Narrow, Focused Questions: Answering specific questions about effectiveness or comparison. Example: Comparing success rates of two IP licensing strategies.
  • Assessing Intervention Quality: Evaluating the effectiveness and quality of specific interventions.
  • Measuring Effectiveness: Quantifying the impact or outcomes of interventions.
  • Homogeneous Literature: Synthesizing studies with similar designs and outcomes (often for meta-analysis).
  • Providing Definitive Conclusions: Aiming to establish the strength of evidence for a specific effect.

Key Frameworks for Scoping Reviews

Several frameworks guide scoping reviews. Combining them can enhance the outcome:

Arksey & O'Malley (2005)

Five-step iterative outline, great for fast-changing fields like fintech. Good for broad mapping (e.g., wearable tech landscape).

Levac et al. (2010)

Extends Arksey & O'Malley, emphasizing stakeholder engagement (vital for TIM collaboration with startups, R&D teams). Example: Engaging accelerators for IoT manufacturing review.

Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI)

Refined methodology for planning and rigor, useful for diverse TIM sources (patents, market analyses). Example: Systematic approach for 3D printing prototyping review.

PRISMA-ScR

Focuses on standardized reporting for credibility and replicability. Example: Ensuring transparency in a blockchain supply chain review.

Recent AI Updates (2020+)

Incorporates AI tools to manage expanding literature volume, speeding up data collection and analysis. Example: Using AI screening for digital entrepreneurship review post-COVID.

Benefits of Using AI for Scoping Reviews in TIM

AI accelerates research, manages large datasets, enhances discovery, and improves organization, leading to more actionable insights in the fast-paced TIM context.

Speed & Efficiency: Rapid screening, automated deduplication.
Enhanced Discovery: Access diverse sources, get intelligent recommendations.
Improved Organization: Topic clustering, automatic tagging.
Scalable Analysis: Handle big data, dynamic updating.
Reduced Human Error: Consistent screening criteria, refinement over time.
Insight Generation: Text summarization, trend detection.
Cost Savings: Streamlined workflows, better resource allocation.

Limitations of Scoping Reviews

While offering breadth, scoping reviews have limitations:

Lack of critical appraisal (may include low-quality studies).
Broad scope may lack depth for specific recommendations.
No formal strength-of-evidence assessment.
Potential for selection bias without strict criteria.
Heterogeneity of studies complicates synthesis.
Lack of meta-analysis (no calculation of effect sizes).
Can be time and resource-intensive.
Difficulty in defining scope for fast-moving fields.
Risk of overlooking grey literature insights.
Challenges in keeping reviews updated in dynamic fields.

Limitations of Using AI Tools

AI tools boost productivity but require caution. Here are key limitations and mitigations:

AI Hallucinations (Citation Errors)

Limitation: AI might invent references or misattribute authors.

Mitigation: Double-check citations (IEEE Xplore, Scopus, Google Scholar); use reference managers (Zotero, Mendeley).

Lack of Contextual Understanding

Limitation: AI may oversimplify complex models or miss nuances.

Mitigation: Rely on human expertise for context; validate AI summaries with original studies/experts.

Bias in AI Content

Limitation: AI may reflect biases (e.g., favoring Anglophone research).

Mitigation: Conduct diverse database searches; triangulate AI insights with multiple sources.

Overreliance on Recent Literature

Limitation: AI might miss foundational works or early research.

Mitigation: Use manual backward citation searching; scan references in key papers.

Difficulty Managing Grey Literature

Limitation: AI often overlooks industry reports, policy briefs, etc.

Mitigation: Supplement with targeted grey literature searches; manually assess non-academic sources.

Inconsistent Handling of Methodologies

Limitation: AI may wrongly classify or equate different study designs.

Mitigation: Manually confirm study designs; engage experts for methodological appraisal.

Ethical Concerns & Plagiarism Risks

Limitation: AI text might resemble existing literature too closely.

Mitigation: Always cite sources; use plagiarism checkers (Turnitin); follow ethics guidelines.

AI Biases in Scoping Reviews

Be aware of biases that can shape AI outputs in TIM research:

Selection Bias

Overrepresenting visible sources (top journals, Silicon Valley) while ignoring others (non-English, emerging regions). Mitigation: Search regional databases/hubs.

Algorithmic Bias

AI amplifying established views (e.g., software focus) and downplaying alternatives (e.g., hardware innovation). Mitigation: Use multiple AI tools, cross-verify.

Confirmation Bias

AI favoring results matching initial search terms (e.g., "successful pilots") while excluding contradictions. Mitigation: Include counter-narratives, critique results.

Citation Bias

AI prioritizing highly cited older work, ignoring newer influential preprints or less-cited niche research. Mitigation: Update searches, include novel studies.

Language & Accessibility Bias

AI focusing on English open-access sources, omitting relevant non-English or paywalled studies. Mitigation: Use multilingual databases, translate key papers.

Data Hallucination

AI merging concepts to create fictitious references or data points from non-existent sources. Mitigation: Verify references/data in standard databases.

Human Oversight is Crucial

In TIM scoping reviews, human experts (researchers, faculty, industry professionals) must actively:

  • Monitor AI outputs for domain accuracy (e.g., correct innovation frameworks).
  • Evaluate study suitability and credibility (e.g., capturing new R&D projects).
  • Intervene when AI misinterprets concepts (e.g., conflating digital entrepreneurship with e-commerce).

Blend AI's efficiency with human contextual knowledge of markets, investors, and regulations to maintain rigor.

Step-by-Step Approach to Grey Literature Searches

Grey literature reveals real-world insights often missed by journals. Follow these steps:

  1. 1 Define scope (reports, whitepapers relevant to your question).
  2. 2 Select keywords & Boolean operators (e.g., "open innovation" AND "accelerator").
  3. 3
    Select sources:
    • Government databases (policy, funding)
    • Think tanks/NGOs (WEF, Brookings)
    • Industry reports (Gartner, CB Insights, PitchBook)
    • Patent databases (USPTO, Google Patents)
    • Conference proceedings (IEEE, Academy of Management)
    • Institutional repositories (theses, working papers)
  4. 4 Search specialized databases (OpenGrey, NTIS, ProQuest, Google Scholar filtered).
  5. 5
    Search competitor info:
    • Official websites (Newsroom, Investor Relations)
    • Press releases
    • SEC filings (EDGAR)
    • Databases (Crunchbase, PitchBook)
    • Assess credibility & bias (cross-reference claims)
  6. 6 Contact organizations/experts directly (agencies, incubators, via LinkedIn).
  7. 7 Filter & evaluate sources based on Authority, Credibility, Relevance, Timeliness.

Part 2: Method to Produce Scoping Reviews

Introduction to the Method

Part 2 outlines a structured, nine-step method for conducting scoping reviews using AI tools, ensuring clarity, transparency, and reliability for TIM researchers.

The 9-Step Process:

  1. Formulate Review Question & Scope: Define using AI-assisted refinement.
  2. Search for Articles: Use AI-driven search strategies.
  3. Select Articles: Screen using AI tools with manual validation.
  4. Extract Data: Utilize AI for extraction and organization.
  5. Analyze & Synthesize Data: Identify themes/gaps with AI categorization.
  6. Interpret Results: Balance AI insights with human evaluation.
  7. Write the Scoping Review: Use AI for drafting with human oversight.
  8. Incorporate Ethical Considerations: Apply guidelines to AI use and integrity.
  9. Disseminate Findings: Leverage AI for summarization and visualization.

Includes a Checklist for Authors and Reviewers.

AI enhances efficiency but does not replace human expertise and critical thinking. Manual oversight is key for accuracy, depth, and theoretical alignment.

Step 1: Formulate the Review Question and Scope

A scoping review needs a broad, exploratory question. AI can assist, but human judgment is vital.

AI's Role
  • Generate question variations (e.g., using ChatGPT prompt: Suggest variations for 'What do we know about AI in mining businesses?').
  • Suggest related topics, synonyms, search terms.
  • Analyze literature to highlight gaps.
  • Identify key authors/papers.
Human Oversight
  • Ensure clarity, feasibility, and alignment with scoping review goals.
  • Validate AI suggestions (avoid overly broad/irrelevant variations).
  • Incorporate theoretical frameworks.

Using the PCC Framework

The Population, Concept, Context (PCC) framework helps structure clear questions.

Example 1: Product Development
  • Population: Startups developing AI-powered products
  • Concept: Challenges in scaling AI prototypes
  • Context: Tech entrepreneurship, venture acceleration
  • Question: What influences scaling of AI prototypes in startups within accelerators?
Example 2: Digital Transformation
  • Population: Mid-sized manufacturing firms
  • Concept: Adoption of Industry 4.0 tech (IoT, AI)
  • Context: North American smart manufacturing
  • Question: What barriers/facilitators influence Industry 4.0 adoption in mid-sized Canadian manufacturing firms?

Alternative Frameworks

Other frameworks like CIMO, ECLIPSE, PEO, SPIDER can also structure questions for different research needs (e.g., complex interventions, qualitative studies).

Define the Scope

  • Breadth vs. Depth: Map emerging area broadly vs. analyze sub-topic deeply.
  • Time: Recent trends (e.g., last 5 years for quantum) vs. historical perspective (e.g., long-term open innovation).
  • Literature Type: Academic, industry reports, patents, empirical vs. conceptual.
  • Geographical Scope: Global vs. regional focus.
  • Stakeholder Perspective: Developers vs. end-users, banks vs. vendors.

Outputs of Step 1

  • Clearly defined research question (using a framework)
  • Review objectives
  • Preliminary list of keywords and search terms
  • Selection of search strategies and tools
  • Defined scope (inclusion/exclusion criteria, sources, disciplines)

Step 2: Search for Articles

A thorough, iterative, and transparent search is essential.

AI's Role
  • Generate keyword variations and Boolean search strings.
  • Identify relevant databases/sources.
Human Oversight
  • Refine AI queries (avoid too broad/narrow results).
  • Select appropriate databases (include grey literature).
  • Adjust Boolean operators manually for precision.

Develop Search Strategy

  • Define key terms, synonyms, operators (AND, OR, NOT).
  • Use AI (e.g., ChatGPT) to suggest Boolean strings for specific engines (Google Scholar).
  • Adapt strategies for different database requirements.
  • Define inclusion/exclusion criteria for filtering later.

Recommended AI Tools for Search

ChatGPT: Generate/refine terms, build Boolean strings, summarize themes.
Consensus: Assess relevance, rank papers by consensus, check agreement levels.
Perplexity: Retrieve from academic & non-academic sources, find connections, access latest research.
Elicit: Extract key insights quickly, prioritize studies, compare side-by-side.
OpenRead: Retrieve papers by meaning, extract relevant parts, summarize complex papers.

Sources of Evidence & Databases

Combine sources for TIM relevance:

  • Academic: Scopus, Web of Science, IEEE Xplore, ACM Digital Library, ABI/INFORM, PsycINFO.
  • Industry: Gartner, McKinsey, CB Insights, ABI Research.
  • Preprints/White Papers: ArXiv, SSRN, institutional repositories.
  • Patents: Google Patents, WIPO, USPTO.
  • Grey Literature: OpenGrey, OAIster, Think Tank Search, government reports.

Example Search Queries

Digital Transformation in SMEs ("digital transformation" OR "Industry 4.0") AND ("SMEs") AND ("barriers" OR "challenges") Filters: Peer-reviewed, last 5 years, English
AI in Venture Capital ("artificial intelligence" OR "AI-driven analytics") AND ("venture capital") AND ("decision-making" OR "risk assessment") Filters: Peer-reviewed, industry reports, last 7 years, global

Manage & Document Search

  • Use reference managers (Zotero, Mendeley) to organize & deduplicate.
  • Track strategies (databases, terms, results) for reproducibility.
  • Refine search iteratively based on initial findings.
  • Document using PRISMA-ScR Flow Diagram (see Step 3).
  • Report search dates, limits, and justify criteria.

Outputs of Step 2

  • Documented search strategy (terms, databases, dates)
  • Collection of relevant articles (in reference manager)
  • Initial PRISMA-ScR flow diagram
  • Refined search query
  • List of excluded articles (with reasons, if applicable at this stage)

Step 3: Select Articles

A structured, transparent two-step screening process (Title/Abstract, Full-Text) using PRISMA-ScR.

AI's Role
  • Classify/rank articles by relevance.
  • Summarize abstracts for quick evaluation.
  • Detect duplicates.
Human Oversight
  • Review inclusion/exclusion criteria manually (AI can misclassify).
  • Assess study quality/relevance critically.
  • Identify hidden biases (e.g., AI favoring recent studies).
  • Use dual human screening for accuracy.

Screening Strategy

1. Title/Abstract Screening

Remove clearly irrelevant studies based on title/abstract using inclusion/exclusion criteria. Use tools like Rayyan AI for rapid filtering. Document changes to criteria.

2. Full-Text Screening

Retrieve and assess full texts of remaining articles against criteria. Use Rayyan AI for sorting/tagging. Justify and document exclusions.

Using Rayyan AI for Selection

  • Import references (RIS, BibTeX).
  • Define criteria within Rayyan.
  • Enable collaborative screening (multiple reviewers).
  • Use prioritization, tagging, and blind review mode features.

Importance of Dual Screening

Dual Screening: Two reviewers independently assess each study against criteria. Discrepancies resolved via discussion or a third reviewer.
  • Minimizes individual bias.
  • Enhances reliability and avoids inadvertent exclusions.
  • Ensures fair decisions, crucial for complex TIM topics.
  • Increases credibility and replicability.

Track and Document

Maintain a PRISMA-ScR flow diagram tracking numbers at each stage (Identified, Screened, Included, Excluded). Keep a record of excluded articles with reasons.

Visually track: Records identified -> Records screened -> Full-text assessed -> Studies included.

Outputs of Step 3

  • Refined set of eligible articles
  • Completed PRISMA-ScR flow diagram
  • List of excluded articles with reasons
  • Updated inclusion/exclusion criteria (if refined)
  • Results of Title/Abstract and Full-Text screening

Step 4: Extract Data

Systematically capture relevant info from selected studies. AI assists, but manual oversight ensures accuracy.

AI's Role
  • Extract key themes, concepts, study characteristics.
  • Summarize findings across papers.
  • Group studies into categories.
Human Oversight
  • Verify extracted info (AI can misinterpret tables/figures).
  • Check contextual accuracy (AI may omit nuances).
  • Ensure methodological consistency (AI might equate qual/quant).
  • Manually cross-check AI accuracy on a sample.

Extract Key Information

Use a structured data extraction form (e.g., Excel, Covidence).

Key data elements:

  • Citation details (Author, Year, Title, DOI)
  • Study type & methodology
  • Population & sample
  • Key concepts & themes
  • Main findings & contributions
  • Identified knowledge gaps

Adjust charting categories iteratively as themes emerge.

AI Tools for Data Extraction & Organization

  • ChatGPT: Basic textual summaries.
  • Elicit, SciSpace: Extract data into customizable tables (Recommended for tables). Add columns for findings, participants, outcomes, etc.
  • Research Rabbit, Litmaps, VOSviewer: Visualize citation networks.
  • Alternative table extractors: Retica, Scite Assistant, Diffbot, OutWit Hub.

Outputs of Step 4

  • Completed data extraction tables/charts
  • Structured dataset of study information
  • AI-generated + manually refined summaries

Step 5: Analyze and Synthesize Data

Organize, summarize, and present findings by mapping literature and grouping themes (not statistical meta-analysis).

AI's Role
  • Summarize key themes.
  • Categorize studies.
  • Generate preliminary thematic maps.
  • Identify patterns and gaps.
Human Oversight
  • Verify thematic accuracy (AI may miss nuances).
  • Ensure methodological consistency (consider study design differences).
  • Assess depth and relevance (AI might prioritize word frequency over concepts).
  • Refine AI categories based on research objectives/theory.
  • Validate AI-identified gaps against expert knowledge.

Descriptive Summary

Summarize findings based on frequency and trends:

  • Number of studies per year, geographic distribution.
  • Common methodologies, research trends.
  • Use tables/figures for visual representation.
  • Example Prompt: Summarize findings of 15 articles on AI in sales management, identify themes/challenges.
  • Triangulate findings from different sources.

Narrative Synthesis

  • Use AI (Custom GPT, SciSpace) to help structure insights.
  • Group studies into conceptual categories based on key themes.
  • Manually validate AI themes before integrating.
  • Identify patterns, relationships, inconsistencies.
  • Ensure synthesized insights align with full texts.
  • Document supporting frameworks, figures, tables, page numbers.

Thematic and Visual Analysis

  • Conduct thematic synthesis to map gaps and clusters.
  • Revisit criteria/extraction if themes are unclear.
  • Address contradictions by consulting original texts.
  • Utilize visualization tools (concept maps, tables, citation networks).

AI-Assisted Analysis

Use AI (Custom GPT, Converse, SciSpace) to:

  • Break down info for structural analysis.
  • Synthesize data from multiple sources.
  • Identify knowledge gaps and suggest research directions.
  • Assist in categorizing studies and creating summary tables.

Outputs of Step 5

  • Structured descriptive summary
  • Thematic synthesis report (narrative)
  • Visual representations (tables, maps, networks)
  • Identified knowledge gaps

Step 6: Interpret Results

Draw meaningful insights, highlight trends, gaps, and implications, maintaining an objective, broad perspective.

AI's Role
  • Generate summaries highlighting themes/trends.
  • Propose applications, policy recommendations, future research areas.
  • Flag inconsistencies and underexplored topics.
  • Compare results and offer preliminary explanations for variations.
Human Oversight
  • Assess contextual accuracy (AI lacks depth/nuance).
  • Ensure logical coherence (AI might draw flawed conclusions).
  • Validate AI implications (AI lacks real-world expertise).
  • Integrate theoretical frameworks (AI doesn't connect findings to theory).
  • Refine interpretations of contradictions/limitations using domain expertise.

Explain Significance & Identify Limitations

  • Relate findings clearly to the review question/objectives.
  • Discuss contributions to the broader field.
  • Identify key takeaways for practice, policy, or research.
  • Acknowledge limitations (data gaps, publication bias, study diversity).

AI-Assisted Interpretation & Caution

Warning: Over-Reliance on AI: While tools like ChatGPT-4o or Jenni can assist, AI summaries can be inaccurate, biased, or lack critical evaluation.
  • Always verify AI insights against full texts.
  • Cross-check sources and ensure no bias is introduced.
  • AI should complement, not replace, human critical thinking and domain expertise.

Maintain a Broad Perspective

Remember, scoping reviews map evidence, not draw firm conclusions. Focus on trends, gaps, and diverse perspectives.

Outputs of Step 6

  • Synthesized discussion of key findings
  • List of research gaps and future directions
  • Limitations section
  • Refined, validated conclusions

Step 7: Write the Scoping Review

Structure the manuscript clearly, use AI as a writing assistant (not replacement), and solicit feedback.

AI's Role
  • Generate initial drafts (summaries, intros, discussion).
  • Suggest logical flow and structure.
Human Oversight
  • Refine AI text for coherence, clarity, academic tone.
  • Ensure academic rigor and apply theoretical frameworks.
  • Check rigorously for citation errors (hallucinations, formatting).
  • Use AI to draft, not finalize.

Solicit Feedback (AI & Experts)

Use an iterative feedback loop:

  • AI Feedback (e.g., Gemini, Perplexity Deep Research): Check clarity, coherence, coverage, thematic consistency.
  • Expert Feedback (Faculty, Peers, Industry): Refine practical relevance, strategic positioning, interpretative depth.

Structure the Manuscript (PRISMA-ScR)

  • Introduction (Background, Objectives, Rationale)
  • Methods (Search, Selection, Extraction, Synthesis)
  • Results (Study characteristics, Findings, Themes)
  • Discussion (Interpretation, Limitations, Gaps, Future Directions)
  • Conclusion (Summary, Implications)

AI-Assisted Writing & Quality Control

  • Use AI (ChatGPT) for initial drafts, Grammarly for polishing/plagiarism check.
  • MANUALLY EDIT for clarity, coherence, alignment, academic tone.
  • Fact-check all AI content against reviewed literature.
  • Conduct multiple revision rounds; cross-check findings against full texts.
  • Clearly document methods and AI usage transparently.

Outputs of Step 7

Step 8: Incorporate Ethical Considerations

Ensure integrity through transparency in data handling, AI usage, bias prevention, and adherence to guidelines.

AI's Role
  • Analyze literature for potential language/methodological biases.
  • Highlight studies lacking transparency or with conflicts of interest.
  • Recommend ethical guidelines (but require human validation).
  • Scan for discussions on ethical implications of the topic.
Human Oversight
  • Evaluate AI-identified biases contextually.
  • Ensure responsible AI use (verify summaries, citations).
  • Verify appropriateness of AI-suggested ethical frameworks.
  • Check for research integrity issues AI might miss (funding, conflicts).
  • Assess inclusivity and fairness in representation.
  • Engage ethical review boards if needed.

Ethical Implications of AI in TIM

Bias & Fairness

AI models can inherit data biases, leading to unequal funding access or flawed insights. Mitigation: Auditing, diverse data, human oversight.

Transparency & Accountability

Opaque AI recommendations hinder trust in high-stakes decisions. Mitigation: Use explainable AI (XAI), document methods clearly.

Data Privacy & Security

AI tools processing sensitive data pose risks (breaches, IP misuse). Mitigation: Robust cybersecurity, anonymization, legal compliance (GDPR).

Impact on Employment & Expertise

Automation may displace roles; over-reliance can reduce critical thinking. Mitigation: Upskilling, AI as collaborator not replacement.

Key Ethical Practices

  • AI Transparency: Clearly disclose and document AI use in all stages.
  • Prevent Plagiarism: Use checkers (Turnitin), manually review AI outputs.
  • Mitigate Bias: Ensure balanced representation of diverse perspectives.
  • Stakeholder Ethics: Get approvals, ensure confidentiality if consulting stakeholders.
  • Compliance: Follow institutional/professional guidelines (e.g., ACM Code of Ethics), consider registering methodology (OSF, PROSPERO).
  • Data Privacy: Comply with regulations, avoid tools storing sensitive data, maintain audit trail.

Outputs of Step 8

  • Transparent AI usage statement
  • Plagiarism and bias report/assessment
  • Documented audit trail of AI methods
  • Compliance checklist for ethical guidelines

2.10 Checklist for Authors and Reviewers

Use this checklist to ensure rigor and quality in your TIM scoping review:

Key Assessment Points:

  • Formulate Question & Scope: PCC defined? Question specific & relevant for TIM? Scope balanced (breadth/depth, time, literature, geography, stakeholders)?
  • Identify & Select Studies: Relevant sources identified (academic, industry, patent, preprint)? Precise search strategy used? AI tools leveraged effectively for search refinement?
  • Screen & Select (Rayyan AI): References uploaded correctly? Inclusion/exclusion criteria applied consistently? Dual screening conducted? AI-assisted prioritization/tagging used appropriately?
  • Extract & Synthesize Data: Key characteristics extracted? Innovation drivers, barriers, success factors identified? Research gaps recognized?
  • Visualize & Report Insights: Concept maps used? AI summaries leveraged (with validation)? Network analysis applied?
  • Translate Research into Action: Opportunities identified (product dev)? Strategic guidance provided (tech adoption)? Competitive intelligence developed? Potential partners identified?
  • Disseminate Findings: Research vignette crafted? Pitch presentation developed? Findings communicated for impact?
  • Ethical Considerations (AI for TIM): Bias mitigation addressed? Transparency/accountability maintained? Data privacy protected? AI balanced with human expertise?
