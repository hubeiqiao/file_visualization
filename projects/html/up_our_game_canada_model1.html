Our Three-Front Strategy

Canada's trade strategy is crafted across three distinct fronts, each with its own timeline and clear objective.

Short Term

Safeguard and Expand U.S. Market Access

Preventing major economic disruptions by fortifying our most critical trade channel.

Medium Term

Build a Strong Domestic Economy

Strengthening our internal market to create stability and innovation needed to fuel broader growth.

Long Term

Diversify Foreign Trade

Expanding into high-growth markets beyond the U.S. to open new avenues for sustainable prosperity.

Each of these fronts is an integral piece of a cohesive strategy—ensuring that today's decisive actions set the stage for tomorrow's resilient growth.

Short Term

Safeguard and Expand U.S. Market Access

The U.S. is Canada's largest trade partner. Maintaining stability in this relationship is crucial for our economic security.

Why This Matters

  • The U.S. is Canada's largest trade partner, making our economic stability closely tied to the health of U.S. trade.
  • Disruptions in this relationship could trigger significant economic instability.
  • Maintaining stability is crucial for preserving business confidence and protecting key industries.

Key Initiatives

Negotiate Targeted Tariff Exemptions

  • • Engage U.S. Trade Representatives for exemptions in key industries
  • • Focus on auto manufacturing, AI technology, clean energy, and critical minerals
  • • Pursue state-level trade diplomacy with Michigan, Texas, and California

Implement Trade Adjustment Mechanism

  • • Negotiate a 'Canada-U.S. Trade Stability Clause'
  • • Secure automatic tariff exemptions for industries with high cross-border dependencies
  • • Establish a joint dispute resolution system

Align with U.S. Security & Economic Priorities

  • • Secure Canada's role in critical U.S. supply chains
  • • Focus on energy, defense, and AI-driven technology
  • • Support U.S. supply chain resilience while preserving strategic flexibility

Strengthen U.S. Business & Congressional Advocacy

  • • Engage with prominent U.S. lobby groups
  • • Target the Chamber of Commerce and National Association of Manufacturers
  • • Work with pro-trade U.S. legislators to underscore Canada's economic importance

Expected Outcomes

  • Preemption of tariff shocks, securing continued U.S. market access
  • Strengthened position as an indispensable trade partner to the U.S.
  • Stability for export-dependent industries, particularly auto, energy, and manufacturing
Medium Term

Build a Strong Domestic Economy

Strengthening our internal market is essential for long-term resilience and reducing vulnerability to external pressures.

Why This Matters

  • Canada's internal market is currently too fragmented, with interprovincial trade barriers limiting the flow of goods and services.
  • When domestic supply chains are limited, we become overly reliant on the U.S. market.
  • Enhancing industrial self-sufficiency acts as a critical buffer against external economic pressures.

Key Initiatives

Eliminate Interprovincial Trade Barriers

  • • Launch a Federal-Provincial Trade Integration Fund
  • • Incentivize provinces to align their regulations
  • • Streamline cross-provincial labor mobility and goods transportation

Develop National Supply Chain Resilience

  • • Leverage AI-driven supply chain monitoring
  • • Reduce overreliance on U.S. suppliers
  • • Strategic investments in logistics hubs and port expansions

Create a Trade Protection Fund

  • • Establish a government-backed fund for Canadian industries
  • • Shield industries from sudden tariff impositions
  • • Offer low-interest loans and targeted tax incentives

Incentivize Advanced Manufacturing & Clean Tech

  • • Drive innovation in AI, EV battery production, and renewable energy
  • • Offer robust R&D incentives
  • • Expand government partnerships with private investors

Expected Outcomes

  • Reduced vulnerability to U.S. protectionism through the elimination of internal trade barriers
  • Boosted GDP growth by removing trade inefficiencies between provinces
  • Decreased reliance on imports through expanded domestic supply chain capacity
Long Term

Diversify Foreign Trade

Expanding into alternative markets enhances our economic stability and strengthens Canada's global position.

Why This Matters

75%+

Over 75% of Canada's exports flow to the U.S.—a concentration that creates high trade vulnerability.

  • This heavy reliance significantly increases our economic risk.
  • By expanding into alternative markets, we can enhance economic stability.
  • Diversification strengthens Canada's global trade position and boosts competitiveness.

Key Initiatives

Strengthen Indo-Pacific & EU Partnerships

  • • Expand CPTPP agreements with Japan, South Korea, and Vietnam
  • • Finalize agreements with Mercosur and ASEAN nations
  • • Secure a Canada–India free trade agreement

Invest in Green Energy & AI Trade

  • • Develop a 'Green Trade Diplomacy Strategy'
  • • Position Canada as a leading global supplier of clean energy and AI technology
  • • Attract investments from EU and Indo-Pacific regions

Strengthen Digital Trade & Financial Services

  • • Negotiate Digital Trade Agreements with EU, ASEAN, and Indo-Pacific partners
  • • Facilitate smoother trade in AI, finance, and e-commerce
  • • Expand trade in quantum computing and blockchain-based finance

Build Long-Term Trade Infrastructure

  • • Expand Canadian ports and high-speed rail systems
  • • Support increased trade with non-U.S. markets
  • • Invest in automated customs processing and AI-driven logistics

Expected Outcomes

  • Reduced reliance on U.S. markets, securing Canada's position as a global trade leader
  • Expanded access to high-growth international markets in Indo-Pacific, EU, and Latin America
  • Canada positioned as a global leader in green energy, AI, and digital services

Our Path Forward

"Our three-front strategy is not just an option—it is a critical imperative for Canada's long-term prosperity."

By safeguarding U.S. market access, building a robust domestic economy, and diversifying our foreign trade, we lay the foundation for both stability and sustainable growth.
